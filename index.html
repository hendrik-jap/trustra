How the score works

Trustra™ uses a weighted scoring model aligned to institutional investor due diligence standards.

How is each document scored?
Each document is worth either 2 points (Critical) or 1 point (standard). Marking "Have it" earns full points. "In Progress" earns 40% of points. "Missing" earns 0. "N/A" removes the item from scoring entirely — it doesn't penalise you.
Why do Critical items count more?
Critical items are documents that investors will always request and that commonly delay or kill deals if missing — for example, IP assignment agreements, cap table, audited financials. They carry 2× weight to reflect their real-world impact on deal closure.
How is the overall score calculated?
Each category (e.g. Financials, Cap Table) has a pre-assigned weight that reflects its importance in the deal type. Your score per category is: earned points ÷ max possible points × 100. The overall score is the weighted average across all categories.
What do the score bands mean?
0–40 Not Ready — Significant gaps. Investors will pause or pass.
41–70 Getting There — Core documents present but gaps remain. Expect investor queries.
71–90 Almost Ready — Strong foundation. Minor items to address before closing.
91–100 Investor Ready — Institutional-grade data room. Ready for serious due diligence.
What checklist standards are used?
SG-focused items align to ACRA (Companies Act), IRAS (tax), MAS (regulatory), and MOM (employment) requirements. International items follow NVCA and institutional PE/VC DD frameworks used globally. Both is recommended for any SG company raising from international investors.
How are category weights assigned?
For Series A: Cap Table & Equity 25%, Financials 25%, Corporate 20%, Legal 10%, Tax 10%, People 10%. For M&A: Financials 30%, Legal 20%, Corporate 15%, Tax 15%, People 10%, Technology 10%. Weights reflect what investors spend the most time on in each deal type.
Can I share my results with an investor?
Yes. Click Copy Link on the results page — the full state of your assessment is encoded in the URL. Anyone with the link can view your exact readiness report. You can also click Download PDF to generate a printable report.
Is my data stored anywhere?
No. All data stays in your browser — nothing is sent to any server. Your progress is saved to local storage so you can continue later, and the shareable link encodes everything in the URL itself using base64.
